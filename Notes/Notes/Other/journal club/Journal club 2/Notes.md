* What are MCL spheroids (MCL-PDLS)

**Introduction**
* Mantle cell lymphoma, rare and aggressive B-cell lymphoma.
* Characterized by CCND1 deregulation
	* Gene, in a family of genes regulating CDKs
	* Mutations of this gene contribute to tumorigenesis
* Recent studies has deciphered the mutational landscape of MCL.
* The mutation effects of MCL often affect DNA damage response, cell proliferation and cell survival.

* *There is cross-talk between MCL-TME and lymph node, with different representation among patients.* A study combining genomic and transcriptomic profiling has revealed patient dependent profiling and outcomes.

* In LN system, interaction between MCL and T cells, stromal cells, secreted factors, maintain MCL viability, and macrophages play a role in poor prognosis, as they can support MCL growth and induce immune exhaustion.

* Many attempts has been made to reform MCL culture systems. No previously generated patient-derived 3D cultures for MCL. There is an urgent need for personalized system cultures for MCLs, establishing disease pathways and immune profile, and allowing T-cell mediated responses.

This work develops a 3D spheroid based model culture for MCL to recreate the TME, called MCL patient derived lymphoma spheroid (MCL-PDLS). This can be later used for immunotherapeutic drug testing and identification of biomarkers.

Confirming published results using this new culture system, like that T-cell immune exhaustion was present. "As expected, the level of immune exhaustion generally increases with the days of culture."

They also tried ibrutinib treatment for MCL response.

"Interestingly, one of the patients included in this study, MCL10, received ibrutinib as 2nd line treatment after relapsing from R-CHOP initial treatment. At first, MCL10 was responsive to ibrutinib achieving a partial response, followed by a new progression 9 months later."

Was this patient dependent or dependent on the culture system they derived?
"These results support the PDLS model as a robust system to predict the response to BTK inhibitors."

"Patient-derived MCL spheroids engage immune activation in response to immune checkpoint inhibitors" Here they tried combinations of ibrutinib treatment with anti-PD1, not showing statistical significance, but still showed benefit from combination.

**Discussion**
"However, it is currently accepted that 3D models better represent cancer biology, signalling pathways, and specially B and T-cell activation, as they are influenced by physical forces that are not recapitulated in 2D cultures"
