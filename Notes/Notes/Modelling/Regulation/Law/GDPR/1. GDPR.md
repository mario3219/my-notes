"*General data protection regulation*"
There is no stated obstacle for healthcare providers to use information-driven care

The rules are formulated in a technology neutral way, so no specific technology is favoured nor disadvantaged.

The rules are however formulated with higher restriction if there's more risks.

**GDPR only applies to personal data, not anonymous data** but it still governs how data is interpreted as anonymous.
* Risk of distinctiveness
Risk of an individual being distinguished from other individuals
* Risk of linkability
Risk of data from different databases being linked to an individual
* Risk of inference
Risk of identifying an individual with the addition of supplementary information.
