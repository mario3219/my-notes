**Expectations**
* Department conventions
* Structural requirements
* Correct referencing
* Expectations for information structure, language, coherence

IMRAD structure
Intro, method, results, discussion
General > specific > specific > general
Why? > How? > What did we find> What does this mean?

In a successful scientific paper, the reader is ale to focus on content without being disturbed by the author's use of language, structural problems or graphic form

**Metatext**
A text writing about what will be in the text
Also helps you structure your own text
Also helps the reader understand your text

**Paragraphs**
One paragraph = one idea
One paragraph = one sentence
All sentences in the paragraph should relate back to the topic sentence

Coherence between paragraphs should be a flow, for example introducing the topic, and next paragraph to be about facts.

In scientific reports, tie the knots of the topic, relate to the topic sentence

**Topic sentence**
Exemplifies, concretizes, specifies, modifies, defines, describes, answers
Always going to be the first sentence
Also acceptable with using questions, however, should be immediately answered.

**Linking words**
Use them! Vary your uses, but be sure what is used when
Linking words are also signposts for your reader, to help the reader pay attention to specific parts

**Make sure to understand the source material**
Read! Write down key words, phrases
<u>How to know you did well</u>
Write a summary in your own words, compare with source
Then it's probably all good to start writing about the source in your text
